Alphabets



Korean Alphabet.



    The Korean alphabet is called Hangeul or Choseongul. This is a board shape that dates back to the past and is still in use today. It was established by the 4th king of the Sejong dynasties with the contributions of dignitaries in the Book of Sages.
    The word system was perfected in 1443 and officially used in 1446 under the main name Coach. In Korea today, Hangeul Day is the anniversary of the birth of the Korean alphabet.
    The Hangeul alphabet was created to help everyone read and write, especially the common people. Instead of using Chinese characters and Korean text as before. Its use helps people get a new language simpler and easier to learn. The main sound set composed by King Sejong played an important role in conveying and disseminating the ideas of leaders in ancient society.
    Until now, Hangul script has become the official language of Korea.





Chinese Alphabet.



    The alphabet of this language is not like other languages. In Chinese documents, the Chinese origin is ideograms, written with a series of symbolic and phonological images. Over time, to meet the needs of use, the Chinese alphabet has had many different variations. You can see the versions of Cantonese, Chinese characters, Han Nom… these are all considered to have originated from Chinese.
    Chinese characters continued to develop until the mid-20th century when simplified Chinese characters were introduced. The purpose of creating simplified characters is to reduce the illiteracy rate. Nowadays, simplified Chinese characters are commonly used in China. Meanwhile, traditional Chinese characters are commonly used in Taiwan and Hong Kong.





Vietnamese Alphabet.



    The Vietnamese alphabet is a system of symbols in Latin letters used to combine meaningful words and record the language in tabular form according to the pronunciation of Vietnamese people.
    Vietnamese alphabet developed based on Latin characters. Vietnamese letters are the first basic premise in language learning. Children from “kindergarten” until entering “first grade” know at least the letters in the alphabet.
    Not only serving for education, the alphabet also helps in recording documents to pass on to generations to come. Therefore, the alphabet plays a huge role in the education industry in Vietnam.





Japanese Alphabet.



    Originally formed, has the longest lifespan and is also the most commonly used. The Japanese believe that using kanji makes reading comprehension really easy and the language has deep meanings.
    However, Kanji also soon revealed its certain limitations, because while Chinese is monosyllabic, Japanese almost has to combine several syllables into a word, and moreover this word when dividing the past, present and future are also different.
    Because of these defects, the Japanese needed to create a new set of characters to overcome these errors. So, 2 alphabets Hiragana and Katakana were born to make up for those shortcomings!





Indian Alphabet.



    Hindi comes from the Hindustani language spoken in North India. It was standardized in the 19th century along with Urdu. These two languages ​​have many similarities and many consider them to be variations of the original language, Hindustani.
    Hindi comes from Indo-European language family, while there are many languages ​​spoken in India and they belong to different language families. The main languages ​​are the Indo-Aryan and Dravidian languages, as well as the Austroasiatic, Tai-Kadai and Sino-Tibetan languages. The country ranks second after Papua New Guinea in the number of languages ​​spoken.





Arabic Alphabet



    The Arabic alphabet (Arabic: عربية ‎ 'abjadiyyah 'arabiyyah) is the writing system used for the writing of many languages ​​in Asia and Africa, such as Arabic and Urdu. After Latin Alphabet, it is the second most widely used alphabet worldwide.[1] The alphabet was first used in writing in Arabic, notably the Quran, the bible of Islam. With the spread of Islam, it came to be used for many languages ​​of language families.
    The basic Arabic alphabet contains 28 characters. Arabic expansions for other languages ​​added and removed some letters, such as the Persian alphabet, Ottoman Kurdish, Sindhi, Urdu, Malaysian, Javanese,Kuryan, Pashto, and Arabic Malayalam, all with extra text as shown below. There is no difference between large and small letters.
    Many letters look similar but are distinguished from each other by a dot (i'jām) above or below the center of the letter (rasm).Dots are an integral part of a word, used to distinguish between characters representing different sounds. For example, phonetic Arabic letters b and t have the same basic shape, but b has a dot below, ب, and t has two dots above, ت.
    Both the printed and handwritten forms of Arabic are cursive, with most of the letters in a word connecting directly to adjacent letters.





Russian Alphabet



    Russian (русский язык, pronounced /ruskʲə: jɪ'zɨk/) is the fourth most popular language in the world and the second largest on the Internet. Therefore, the chances of getting a good job while studying Russian are very high.
    Many people think that Russian is a difficult language to learn because of its diverse grammar. However, the pronunciation and spelling in Russian are quite similar to Vietnamese, so we have a slight advantage when learning this language.

Cambodian Alphabet



    Khmer language also called Cambodian is the language of the Khmer people. Besides, it is also the official language of Cambodia. With about 16 million speakers, it is the second most popular language in the Austroasiatic family (after Vietnamese). Khmer is significantly influenced by Sanskrit and Pali through Hinduism and Buddhism. Especially in the area of ​​royal language and religion.
    The colloquial Khmer had an impact on and was also influenced by Thai, Lao, Vietnamese, and Cham languages. Due to geographical proximity and enduring cultural influence, a Southeast Asian language region is created. It is also the earliest recorded Mon–Khmer language and has the oldest writing system. Before Mon and long before Vietnamese because Old Khmer was the language of the kingdoms of Chan Lap, Angkor and, perhaps, Funan.





Taiwan Alphabet



    Taiwanese Hokkien is often referred to as Taiwanese for short. This is a variation from the Minnan-Chinese language. This language is spoken by 70% of Taiwanese people on a daily basis. The rest use Mandarin Chinese (Mandarin).
    Taiwanese can be considered as the dialect of China. Can also be considered an independent language. Because this language has different syllables, unlike Mandarin Chinese.





Greek Alphabet



    The alpha beta Greek alphabet (“Ελληνικό αλφάβητο” – Elleniká alphabeto) is a 24-letter system used to write Greek. The Greek alphabet was inherited from the Phoenician alphabet, which was the basis for many other European and Middle Eastern alphabets, including the Latin alphabet.
    Since the Greek alphabet often appears in mathematical formulas and symbols, it is easy to memorize the alphabet.





German Alphabet



    German is a language with a strong difference between spoken and written languages. Written language uses a lot of embedded relative clause structure, but it is almost never used in everyday communication. In everyday communication, a relative clause (also known as a subordinate sentence, nebensatz) is often used and is always said with a relative word. The reduction of any grammatical structure even in the spoken style (such as the reduction of relational words as in English) is also considered as grammatically incorrect.
