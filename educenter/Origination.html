Trial of Ly Tu

Ly Tu was assigned by Emperor Qin to unify the text into one style and was also considered to be the foundation for calligraphy.

Ly Tu (李斯, 284 BC - 208 BC) was an ancient Chinese politician who served as prime minister under the reigns of Qin Shi Huang and Qin Er Shi. He was a man of great merit in the unification of vassals by Qin Shi Huang, turning China into a centralized monarchy, unified in writing, measurement and ideology.

Background
Ly Tu is from Thuong Sai land of Chu country
He was born in the Warring States period
He and Han Phi were both students of Tuan Tu.
When he was young, he worked as a small mandarin in the district, was a secretary in the commune's clerical administration, and since then he has cultivated the will to do politics.

Set up a career

In 247 BC, when he arrived in the Qin state, when he met the death of Qin Trang Tuong Vuong, Ly Tu asked to be a follower of Van Tin Hau La Bat Vi, the prime minister of the Tan state. Bat Vi is considered a sage who is used as the "Lang" mandarin. Because of that, Ly Tu had the opportunity to travel, and Qin Vuong Chinh made Tu the chief historian, and then the guest. Among the 3,000 "guests" of La Buwei, Ly Tu quickly became the most prominent.

In the ninth year of the 9th dynasty, through many events, Li Shu took power in his hands and changed his personality, and he harmed (Han Fei) causing his death. and this is also the historical tragedy of the times.

Prime Minister of the Qin Dynasty (Li Tu)

Tu is related to the family lieutenant. For more than 20 years, the Qin state annexed all of its vassal states and unified China. King Qin became emperor and made Ly Tu prime minister. The Qin Dynasty flattened the vassal castle, ordered the districts and districts to melt weapons, meaning they were no longer in use. Heeding Ly Tu's will, Qin Shi Huang did not give land to anyone, even if it was only a few feet long, did not establish a royal family as a king, and a public god as a vassal to avoid repeating the story of countries fighting.

Through his words, Qin Shi Huang obeyed him and confiscated all the books of the Classics, the Classics, and the books of the Huns, leaving only the books of medicine, fortune-telling, and planting books, avoiding the use of old affairs. to consider this life. Ly Tu also helped the king to make laws, unify the literature, measure, build palaces and villas throughout the country, defeat the Xiongnu.

His death

After doing anti-war, the emperor's power was due to treason. Ly Tu Một nămand his son were subjected to a torture called the "five punishments" that he invented[2] and the three of them were killed together, with their heads covered at the city gate. It was 208 BC, when he was about 60 years old.

A year after Ly Tu died, Trieu Cao forced Nhi The to commit suicide.[3] King Qin Tu Anh ascended the throne, killed Trieu Cao, then Luu Bang invaded Qin, captured Tu Anh, later Tu Anh was killed by Xiang Yu. The Qin Dynasty was destroyed.

- Chinese characters have been used in Vietnam since at least 2000 years ago. The independent feudal and monarchy dynasties in Vietnam all considered Chinese characters, Chinese literature as the state administrative language, literary language... Even under the Nguyen Dynasty, the Vietnamese court also claimed to be the next subject. cultural heritage, orthodox Han language, than the Northern Qing. Chinese characters have lost their unique position, giving way to the national language since the beginning of the 20th century, marked by the abolition of the entrance exam at the end of the Nguyen Dynasty. Although it is no longer respected as in the past, Chinese characters continue to be used by the Democratic Republic of Vietnam (North) and the Republic of Vietnam (South) in many official cases. Only after the Socialist Republic of Vietnam was established (1976), especially after the Vietnam - China war took place (1979), did Chinese characters officially step out of the historical arena and gradually become a things "strange letters", assigned with many "strange" values.''

Although the level of calligraphers is not always high, these classes of people have made quite an important contribution in human history in terms of beautifying the writing, invisibly making the text more attractive. However, in many historical periods, those who abuse the title of calligraphers for their own profit have inadvertently destroyed the beauty of calligraphy.

Influence of countries in the world

Asia Oriental calligraphy some details about calligraphy [ East Asia] (Chinese: 東亞書法, East Asian calligraphy) is the art of beautiful handwriting formed from the practice of writing Chinese characters. People use brush, dot Chinese ink, write Chinese characters on fine paper or silk fabric, in different styles. In Asian calligraphy art, there are 5 writing styles: Chan (also known as Khai), Trien, Le, Hanh and Thao with their own specific rules of lines and expressions.

Chinese calligraphy is the Chinese writing that was raised to an art and had a profound influence on neighboring countries such as Japan, Korea, and Vietnam. When brushes, paper and ink were invented, kanji began to be written in large and small strokes. Since the Han Dynasty, Chinese characters have stabilized in font style and type of strokes. The same character, but the Chinese characters have 5 main writing styles (called shōji ) : The seal (including the great seal 大篆 and the minor seal 小篆), the lishu 隸書, the Khaishu 楷書, the Xingshu 行書, and the draft letter 草書.
The above typefaces

Seal 篆書 (Seal Letter). When Qin Shi Huang unified China, he ordered Prime Minister Ly Tu to unify the script, that is, to unify the scripts between the previous countries from the great seal to the minor seal.

Letter 隸書 (letter rule). A common form of official correspondence, this typeface was very popular between the 3rd and 2nd centuries BC.

The word revelation 楷書 (revelation letter or letter 正書). It is a modification of the script and began to be popular in the 3rd century CE. This is the official, standard, recognizable, most readable and still the most popular type of Chinese writing today.

The kanji 行書 (letter). It is a shorthand form of the word Revelation, used in intimate documents (such as letters) and pictures. The word kanji began to be popular in the 2nd century. When written quickly, the word Khai could be shortened by a stroke or two to form a form called han Khai (行楷). Likewise, the kanji will turn into cursive (行草). The very famous calligraphy work Lanting Xie Tu (蘭亭集序) by Wang Xizhi (王羲之) of the Jin Dynasty was written with kanji.

Cursive (cursive letter). Is the fastest written form, liberal style. With Chinese characters, when written normally according to the Khai script, you have to write a lot of strokes, but with cursive letters, you can write with one stroke. Many characters can be written consecutively with a single stroke, for example Huaisu's wild draft (狂草) (crazy cursive) (懷素, circa 730-780).

Westernization

    Western calligraphy (also known as Calligraphy) has a different style from Asian calligraphy. The lettering is straightened according to standards and proportions instead of the common cursive style in Asian calligraphy.

    Western calligraphy can be expressed by many means: iron pen, brush, ruler, compass, eke...

Arab World

    Arabic calligraphy is an aspect of Islamic art that has developed in tandem with Islam and the Arabic language.

    Islamic calligraphy (calligraphy in Arabic is khatt ul-yad (خط اليد) and in Persian Khosh-Nevisi (خوشنویسی) has evolved with Islam and the Arabic language. Arabic letters so some people refer to it as "Arabic calligraphy." However, the term "Islamic calligraphy" is a more appropriate term as it includes all calligraphic works of the writers. Islamic calligraphy belongs to different national cultures, such as Persian or Ottoman calligraphy, from Al-Andalus in medieval Spain to China, India.

    Arabic/Persian calligraphy is related to the Islamic art decorations on the walls and ceilings of mosques as well as on the pages of scriptures. Contemporary artists of the Muslim world have learned from the legacies of calligraphy to use in their works.

    Instead of evoking something related to the reality of speech, for Muslims, calligraphy is an expression of the noblest art - the art of the spiritual world. Calligraphy has become the holiest form of Islamic art because it provides a link between the Muslim languages ​​and Islam. The Bible of Islam, the Koran, has played an important role in the development of the Arabic language, which by extension is Arabic calligraphy. Aphorisms and complete passages in the Koran are still living sources for Arabic calligraphy.
