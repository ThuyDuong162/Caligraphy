The famous 'baby man' in Saigon

Holding a brush in his hand, looking directly at the person sitting opposite, 13-year-old boy Tran Thien Phu thought for a moment and then dabbed ink, "dancing" on paper.
Receiving the calligraphy painting that Thien Phu gave her, Ms. Thu Trinh, 47 years old, from District 12 was surprised: "I am so surprised that such a small boy can write calligraphy so fluently. Especially, just glanced at my face for a few seconds, but gave me a sentence that matched my thoughts and mood right now."
The words that the boy "gives" to Trinh are "The family is full of peace, wealth and fortune come, full of blessings". Touched, but the woman did not dare to sit more because behind her, nearly 50 other people were queuing.

Born in a family where no one knew how to write calligraphy, from a young age, every spring, boy Thien Phu (Tan Binh district) was often taken by his mother to Tao Dan park, Ong Do street to visit. When he met the scene where the old man was holding a brush "for words", the boy was very fascinated. "I like to see the way the old man gives the pen strokes, their hands are very flexible and soft," recalled Phu.
During the first year of the year, when he knew how to write letters, Thien Phu asked his mother to let him learn to write calligraphy. Although a bit surprised, but thinking that this subject is like swimming, playing chess, helping children develop intellectually, Mrs. Nguyet took her child to register.
So, three times a week, Mrs. Nguyet took her children to class. In the early days, I just practiced writing each letter, gradually combining it into single words and compound words. However, like many elementary school students, he often misspelts.
I love calligraphy, but Phu only sees this as an activity to help me relax, after studying, I just started to practice writing. In order to know and understand many sentences, Thien Phu, besides practicing writing, also read many books about grape characters. Wherever I go, whenever I see something good, I remember to go home to find out more.
At the end of first grade, Phu was introduced to the pagoda by a nun who studied in the same calligraphy class to exchange and give letters on the full moon day. "There were also many other masters that day, but when I saw a young child sitting and writing hard, sweat drenched in his shirt, he still wouldn't stop. Everyone was curious and lined up to ask for letters," Mrs. Nguyet said. tell.After that session, the abbot of the temple called to the newspaper, the "arbitrary" box received a lot of money from everyone supporting the "baby man". Mrs. Nguyet asked her son, Thien Phu said: "For a long time, I have wished to do charity work, but I have not had the opportunity. Now, if I have money, I will donate it to the temple".
Since then, the good news has spread far and wide, although Thien Phu has not been able to write beautiful calligraphy, he has been invited by many temples and places to give letters. Every Tet holiday from the 1st to the full moon of January, I am always present at Pho Quang Pagoda, Phu Nhuan District to show people how to go to the temple.

In the past three years, his calligraphy has become much more beautiful. In particular, I like to look at the faces of the guests and then "give words" according to how I feel about that person.
Once, on New Year's Day, my mother told Thien Phu to choose words to express goodness and joy in the new year. But when I met a woman dressed elegantly, looking rich, I gave a sentence as if people were in debt: "Sadness let the wind blow, life's story is so why bother". It seemed that the calligraphy made the woman uncomfortable, but Phu had just finished writing it, she cried and said: "This boy wrote my heart". Standing for a while talking to Mrs. Nguyet, the woman said that she was in debt to many people, so she was sad in her heart.
Another time, a man with austere appearance asked for words. At a glance, Thien Phu wrote on the paper the word "Causal", with the sentence "I think there is no loss. Sowing causes and picking results, I also collect".
Mrs. Nguyet thought, this sentence is completely inappropriate, the man looks so miserable, where is the money to give to others or to do charity. Suddenly, the customer asked for words to hug his face and cry, hire someone to frame him to bring him home. Arriving there, Mrs. Nguyet saw that his house was a very large villa. Despite being rich, he lived a simple life and often helped many poor students.
"Phu told me not to force me to write like this anymore, I had a headache. After that, I let my son decide for himself," said Mrs. Nguyet.
Although he likes poetry and writing, Phu is the representative of the school to compete for good students in math and assemble robots. I like the natural subjects the most. Thien Phu's dream is to study in the field of information technology.
"Looking at me, everyone says I'm like a young old man, old before my age, but only when I focus on writing. Letting go of the pen, I get along with friends like any other child," said Mrs. Nguyet.
