Children

Introducing calligraphy for kids

    Calligraphy also does not specify what age to learn, including children. Of course, before learning calligraphy, they must also be fluent in reading and writing, and must understand the basic meaning of the words they write."

    Talking about teaching calligraphy to children, Mr. Hoa Nghiem showed great interest: "At first, I was also a bit shy and confused because I saw that the children were too young. There were students who were just in 2nd and 3rd grade. Even after a period of attachment, the children's innocence, innocence, attentiveness, and concentration made me love them more and more and more and more excited. enjoy your job".

    According to teacher Hoa Nghiem, compared with adults, the curriculum for children is basically not much different but also has its own points suitable for the age and level of each child.

    Vietnamese calligraphy currently has 5 main typefaces, in which the typeface Chan Phuong (Truong Tu) is a simple, clear, easy-to-read writing style, similar to lowercase letters, very suitable for young learners.

    Children will learn from the most basic lines, ordinary letters, and practice again and again until they are proficient, then move on to a new stage with simple and close folk songs and proverbs in their language. life.

Before learning to write, children always learn the meaning of words to understand the beautiful words in each sentence, thereby conveying the best way through their pen. Calligraphy is not simply an art of beautiful handwriting but also a profoundly educational subject that has a profound impact on people's hearts and minds. Learners practice both letters and the mind, so it will have a very good influence on the process of forming a child's personality. According to calligrapher Kieu Quoc Khanh, who has successfully organized many calligraphy - national painting courses, the number of children studying this art is increasing day by day.

In which, some go to school according to the wishes of their parents, some by the children themselves. But at the age of 10 - 15 like you, most of them just love calligraphy emotionally in the way that they find it interesting, enlightened, and learn when they find it strange. (Actually, not only children but many adults also attend for the same reason). But after the process of learning and training, some students have really found a passion in calligraphy, considering it a true art for them to pursue long-term.
